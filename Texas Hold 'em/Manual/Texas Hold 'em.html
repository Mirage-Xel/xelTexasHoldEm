To begin, call the big blind by betting $2 using the buttons on the module. After doing so, three community cards will be dealt in the middle of the module; edeal them from your deck as well.

For each other player, raise the bet from the outstanding bet by the number of player for which best poker hand they can make from their hole cards and the community cards is worst than the best you can make.

After betting, some players will fold; their hole cards do not to be redealt. A new community card will be dealt; repeat the previous steps, including dealing the other players' hole cards. The module will solve after correctly betting four times, including calling the big blind.

Below is an explanation of poker hands and how they are ranked, from highest to lowest. Aces are high unless stated ortherwise.

  • Straight flush: A hand that countains five cards of sequential rank, all of the sme suit. Aces can rank either high or low, but cannot simultaneously rank both high and low. Each straight flush is ranked by the rank of its highest-ranking card.
  • Four of a kind: A hand that contains four cards of one rank and one card of another rank (the kicker). Each four of a kind is ranked first by the rank of its quadruplet, and then by the rank of its kicker.
  • Full house: A hand that contains three cards of one rank and two cards of another rank. Each full house is ranked first by the rank of its triplet, and then by the rank of its pair.
  • Straight: A hand that contains five cards of sequential rank, not all of the same suit. See above for rules on aces and rank.
  • Three of a kind: A hand that contains three cards of one rank and two cards of two other ranks (the kickers). Each three of a kind is ranked first by the rank of its triplet, then by the rank of its highest-ranking kicker, and finally by the rank of its lowest-ranking kicker.
    • Two pair: A hand that contains two cards of one rank, two cards of another rank and one card of a third rank (the kicker). Each two pair is ranked first by the ranks of its pairs from high to low, and then by the rank of its kicker.
  • Pair: A hand that contains two cards of one rank and three cards of three other ranks (the kickers). Each pair is ranked first by the rank of its pair, then by the ranks of its kickers from high to low.
  • High card: A hand that does not fall into any other category. Each high card hand is ranked by the ranks of its cards from high to low.
